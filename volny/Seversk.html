Have you ever heard about „Seversk“ - my newest artificial language?

 

Seversk is  the newest North Slavonic artificial language. Noun ,sever‘ means a ,north‘ and ,sk‘ is a typical suffix for language names. ,Seversk‘ or also ,Jåzaht Severøn‘ is a successor to ,Slavëni‘. Written form of Seversk is almost phonetical and without any special unusual diacritics (letter ,Å‘ = 0197 and ,å‘ = 0229‘ & ,Ø‘ = 0216 and ,ø‘ = 0248‘ & ,Š‘ = 0138 and ,š‘ = 0154‘). You may type ,ao‘ instead of ,å‘ & ,oe‘ instead of ,æ‘ & ,sj‘ instead of ,š‘.

P R O N U N C I A T I O N :

V o w e l s .  Generally, Seversk has 8 vowel qualities: A Å E I O Ø U Y. Almost all of them can be either short or long; only the ‚å‘ letter is never short and its sound is somewhere between long ‚a‘ and ‚o‘, being dull, back and rounded. Letter ‚ø‘ is  standard Indo-European mid central vowel ‚schwa‘  - same like ‚a‘ in English ‚attention‘. The ‚y‘ is close front rounded - same like in Norwegian words ‚dyrke‘ or ‚ty‘. There is no need to differenciate vowels' length, but one principe has general validity anyway: Vowels are long almost only in stressed syllables.

C o n s o n a n t s .  Seversk has 18 consonants - less than any other Slavonic language - if only for the reason that some usual Pan-Slav sounds like [ch] [kh] [zh] are missing. When they are in final position, the voiced consonats » b, d, g, h, v, z « are pronounced like their voiceless counterparts » p, t, k, [ch], f, s «. In consonantal clusters there is an assimilation to the last consonant in the group - progressive assimilation.
